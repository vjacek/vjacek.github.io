Polybius

March 7, 2026

Colin Armstrong

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

This book gets 2 massive blunders out of the way early, then goes on to be a really fun and fast paced lite-horror thriller where the teenage protagonists do some really smart things, a few very stupid things, and an ending very reminscent of Breaking Bad: noone makes it out alive. Not really alive, anyways. I’m not usually very strict on the datestamp of these reviews, but this one I know for a fact has to be written as completed on the 7th, because I stayed up past 1am last night to finish it.

I’ll get the blunders out of the way early too:

  • There are no plungers on arcade cabinets. Those are found on pinball machines.
  • Whats the first “fun fact” they tell you when you tour a clean room? Let’s all say it together… “The breeze is because positive air pressure keeps the dust out” (class: ohhhh that’s actually really smart). Colin must have missed this day of high school, because in the book the clean room is pulling a vacuum, making it hard to open a door from the outside.

Whatever, the book is still pretty good.

It’s a fun thriller that gets just up to my line on the horror parts.

As the plot starts to deepen, instead of saying “no participating in this super cool mystery” to the teenagers, the sheriff gives caution but also gives sound advice since they’re going to jump in and investigate anyway.

I do wish someone would have just smashed the game earlier when it was even a possibility that it was the cause of the town’s problems. But those poor suckers in the book keep trying to play by the rules. I’ve come to understand that rules are made up by yesterday’s winners to keep themselves in power, and mostly have absolutely no scientific or moral basis.

The writing is a little awkward because it’s so strictly 3rd person. The main character has a very personal relevation that her father is not someone to look up to, and it’s like “Andi realized that Devon is a loser”.

Spoiler I figured out why Andi isn’t affected on page 163, a few tens of pages before it’s made clear. The clue was that the innards of Polybius are described as DLP and she is colorblind.

While writing this review, I just found out that there’s an urban legend of the game too, which makes it even cooler.

EDIT: Reading the the DLP wiki is worth it just to learn that Wobulation is a real word.
