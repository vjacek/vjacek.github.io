I can usually tell how I'll end up rating a book based on how long it takes me to complete it. This book took over a month and was frequently a real slog. The fun magic and discovery of a world wasn't enough in this case to overcome the boring parts.

This book really suffers from being filled with [Wild Goose Chases](2023-10-11-The-Wise-Mans-Fear.md). There's so much traveling from one place to another without any real consequence or need for the travel to happen in a certain order.

As one example, new Jimmy and his crew get captured by a band of elves living in an exclave near the Peaks of the Quor. Jimmy escapes and returns to the elven homebase at Elvandar for help, at which point Tomas returns to the Peaks of the Quor with Jimmy riding upon a dragon. Tomas quickly assess that the exclave evles need reincorcements, so he takes the dragon back to Elvandar, in order to return with reinforcements back to the Peaks of the Quor. That's 4 trips in total, and it feels like they only exist to eat up pages of content.

In a separate example, Pug's wife Miranda is great at teleporting herself all over the place, but of course she's the only one who can do it. She spends the majority of the book teleporting all over picking up small bits of information here and there without doing much to advance the story. All the other magicians are relegated to using special teleportation rooms like peasants.

There's also a new writing rule that has to be coined as a result of this book:

_Never say "I'll Tell You Another Time"_

THIS IS A BOOK! THIS IS THE OTHER TIME!! The story is complete and ostensibly someone survived to write it down, so we've got all the time in the world now! You can't use this as a subsitute for not answering good questions that characters in the story ask. This just means that you were being lazy in the writing, or didn't come back and actually do the editing needed to explain different facets of the story. This really drives me nuts.
