DID NOT FINISH

The most generous thing I can say about this book is that it's printed on nice heavy paper. That means that for the same thickness of book, the story will be much shorter. Which is good, because the book is trash. It's the story of a elite-level incel who daydreams about the one time a girl touched him, and is therefore happy to keep working in a dead-end job to stay close to her.

Critically, in just 50 pages, there were a ton of incredibly stuipid editing errors:

- Several places just have missing words that make the reader confused and stumble
- Characters don't know the difference between Sony (the brand) and Sunny (a nickname)
- "herd of orcas". Why yes, I have, in fact groups of them are called pods.
- The girl's name is Eva, but it's misspelled as Eve.

Much as [Rabbits](/content/book-reviews/2024-11-8-Rabbits.md) did, _Head Cleaner_ helpfully establishes itself as work of fiction early on, when the main characters debate how to make [The Matrix](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0133093/?ref_=ls_t_2) better; a feat which cannot be done as it's one of the few perfect movies.

Finally, it tries to say "...but for reasons unexplainable...".

No no no, this is called a book. You've got several hundred pages to work with. Do some explaining. Especially when it shows us the characters motivations

Total fail.
